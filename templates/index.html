About this Pipeline

How items appear in the list

Items are ingested automatically via a Jira webhook. When a new issue is created in Jira that matches the configured JQL filter, Jira sends it to this app's webhook endpoint. The app downloads any attached video, transcribes it, extracts screenshots, then sends everything to Claude AI for analysis. Processed items persist in a local database and survive restarts.

How items move between tabs

Ready for Review — Claude determined there was enough information to generate a complete ADO bug report. The item waits here until you review and manually submit it, or re-process it.

Submitted — You clicked "Review & Submit" and confirmed submission. The ADO work item was created and the item is moved here permanently.

Needs More Info — Claude found the Jira ticket lacked the required detail (repro steps, expected vs. actual behavior, etc.) to generate a quality bug report. Claude posts a comment back to the Jira ticket listing what's missing. Once the reporter updates the ticket, you can click Re-process to try again.

Errors — Something failed during processing (video download, transcription, ADO API, etc.). The error message is shown so you can investigate.

How confidence is calculated

Confidence is not a code formula — it's a 0–100 score that Claude assigns based on how complete and unambiguous the ticket information is. A high score means Claude could clearly identify the bug title, repro steps, expected behavior, and actual behavior. A low score means the information was sparse or ambiguous but still technically sufficient to attempt a report.

What confidence is used for

Confidence is purely informational — it does not block submission. It appears as a color-coded bar (green ≥ 80%, yellow 50–79%, red < 50%) and can be used to filter the table. Low-confidence items are worth reviewing more carefully before submitting, as Claude may have made assumptions to fill in missing detail.
